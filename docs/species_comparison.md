Species Comparison

Purpose

Evaluate fish species as nutrient generators in aquaponic systems.

---

🐟 Piranhas (Experimental)

piranhas

Pros:

- High protein metabolism → strong nutrient output
- Flexible diet
- Unique experimental behavior

Cons:

- Aggressive / territorial
- Cannibalism risk
- Stress-sensitive
- Unstable population dynamics

---

🐟 Tilapia (Baseline)

tilapia

Pros:

- Extremely hardy
- Fast reproduction
- Stable waste production
- Industry standard

Cons:

- Less “experimental”
- Can overpopulate

---

🐟 Common Carp

common carp

Pros:

- Tolerant of poor conditions
- Stable biomass
- Easy to manage

Cons:

- Slower growth
- Lower nutrient spikes

---

Comparison Table

Trait| Piranhas| Tilapia| Carp
Stability| Low| High| High
Aggression| High| Low| Low
Waste Output| High| Medium| Medium
Ease of Use| Low| High| High
Experimental Value| High| Medium| Medium

---

Recommendation

- Use tilapia/carp for baseline calibration
- Introduce piranhas for:
  - Edge-case behavior
  - High-variance experiments

---

Insight

Species choice changes not just output—but predictability, which is critical for system modeling.
