Nitrogen Cycle in Aquaponics

Overview

The nitrogen cycle is the core transformation engine of the system.

---

Stages

1. Ammonia Production (NH₃)

Source:

- Fish waste
- Uneaten feed

Toxicity:

- Highly toxic to fish at low concentrations

---

2. Nitrite Formation (NO₂⁻)

Bacteria: Nitrosomonas

Reaction:

NH₃ → NO₂⁻

Toxicity:

- Still toxic, but less than ammonia

---

3. Nitrate Formation (NO₃⁻)

Bacteria: Nitrobacter

Reaction:

NO₂⁻ → NO₃⁻

Properties:

- Relatively non-toxic
- Primary plant nutrient

---

System Behavior

Condition| Effect
High protein feed| ↑ Ammonia → ↑ Nitrate
Low bacteria| Ammonia spike (danger)
Stable system| Smooth nitrate production

---

Key Ratios

- Feed input ≈ nitrogen output driver
- Plant uptake must balance nitrate production

---

Failure Modes

- Ammonia spike → fish death risk
- Nitrite accumulation → system instability
- Nitrate overload → algae / plant imbalance

---

Monitoring Targets

Parameter| Safe Range (General)
NH₃| ~0 ppm
NO₂⁻| ~0 ppm
NO₃⁻| 20–150 ppm
pH| 6.5–7.5

---

Insight

Feed does not directly feed plants.
It feeds the nitrogen cycle, which feeds the plants.
